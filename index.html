Mathieu Chanavat

I am a graduate student in math and computer science. My main interests are category theory, homotopy theory and their applications to computer science. I'm currently doing an intership under the supervision of Pierre-Louis Curien where I study generalizations in toposes of this paper about cubical type theory and how we can give comptutational content to the univalence axiom. I'll be interested in starting a PhD in a related area by fall 2023.

After three years of "classe préparatoire" (intense classes of math and physics), I went for three years at École Centrale de Lille were I studied general computer science, AI and data science. During these three years, I also obtained a bachelor and a master of mathematics, focused on probabilites and functional analysis. During the year 2021-2022, I was a student at the LMFI Master where I developed my skills about theoretical computer science, category theory, homotopy theory, and logic in general. Here's my CV.

  1. Category theory in Coq (2022) github
    Final project of the LMFI's course 'Functional programming and formal proofs in Coq' where a student and I (re)implemented basics of category theory in Coq.

  2. Loops of Csörgő Type and the AIM Conjecture (2021) pdf
    Summer '21 internship. I worked on the AIM conjecture and tried to develop a computational approach to find Csörgő loops (a certain kind of non-assiociative structure) of size less than 128. Interestinlgy enough, the AIM conjecture was solved (independantly of me!) by Michael Kinyon and Bob Veroff during my internship. Some of my code can be found here. You can find here the slides of a talk I gave during this internship.
